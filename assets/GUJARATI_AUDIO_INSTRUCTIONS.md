## Audio Requirements

### Voice Content
**Gujarati Text:**
```
"Chal!
Aaje aapde aksharo lakhata shikhiye!"
```

**English Translation:**
"Come!
Today let's learn to write letters!"

### Voice Specifications
- **Language:** Gujarati
- **Speaker:** Female voice (child-friendly, warm tone)
- **Age:** Young adult (20-30 years) with clear pronunciation
- **Tone:** Cheerful, encouraging, friendly
- **Pace:** Medium-slow (clear for ADHD children)
- **Duration:** 3-4 seconds
- **Format:** MP3
- **Quality:** 128 kbps or higher

### Style Guidelines
- ✅ Clear, natural pronunciation
- ✅ Warm, welcoming tone
- ✅ Slightly energetic but not overwhelming
- ✅ Native Gujarati speaker
- ❌ No background music
- ❌ No echo or heavy effects
- ❌ Not too fast or rushed
- ❌ Not robotic or text-to-speech

## How to Obtain This Asset

### Option 1: Record with Native Speaker
1. Find a native Gujarati speaker (female, clear voice)
2. Use a smartphone voice recorder or:
   - Audacity (free desktop software)
   - GarageBand (Mac)
   - Voice Memos app (iOS)
3. Record in a quiet room
4. Have speaker say: "Chal! Aaje aapde aksharo lakhata shikhiye!"
5. Export as MP3
6. Rename to `gujarati-intro.mp3`

If human voice is not immediately available:

**Note:** Human voice is STRONGLY preferred for ADHD children.

## Script Details

### Pronunciation Guide (Romanized)
```
Chal! (chul) - short, energetic
Aaje (aa-jay) - clear
aapde (aap-day) - friendly
aksharo (uk-sha-ro) - clear enunciation
lakhata (la-kha-ta) - emphasized
shikhiye! (shee-khee-yay) - encouraging ending
```

### Recording Tips
1. **Take several takes** (choose the best one)
2. **Smile while speaking** (makes voice warmer)
3. **Speak to a child** (imagine addressing a 6-year-old)
4. **Add a slight pause** after "Chal!" for emphasis
5. **End with upward inflection** on "shikhiye!" (encouraging)

## Testing

2. Is it clear enough for children?
3. Is the pace appropriate (not too fast)?
4. Would it encourage a child with ADHD?
